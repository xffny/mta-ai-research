# AI Ads on the MTA

A personal field study tracking AI company advertising across the New York
City subway system.

## What this is

A submission-based database and frontend for cataloging AI-related ads that
appear on the MTA: buses, subway cars, station placements, and digital
displays. Each entry captures details about the ad itself, the company
behind it, and the placement context.

## Why

In early 2025 I started noticing a wave of AI advertising rolling into NYC
public transit. First Genspark, then a steady stream of others. As someone
with a marketing background working adjacent to the AI industry, I wanted
to see whether personal observation, collected systematically, could
surface patterns about which AI companies are betting on B2C out-of-home
advertising and what that strategy actually looks like in practice.

The longer why lives on the project site.

## What gets tracked

**Placement**
- Type: digital or static
- Size and format (significant variance, even between cars on the same line)
- Line(s) and stations where observed
- Date of observation

**Creative**
- Byline and headline copy
- Typography (AI-adjacent fonts are weirdly recognizable once you start looking)
- Color palette
- Imagery
- Industry jargon used

**Company**
- Product category and apparent target market (B2B, B2C, prosumer)
- HQ location
- Funding stage and round
- Product-market fit signals
